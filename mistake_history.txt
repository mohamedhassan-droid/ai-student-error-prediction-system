6. Targeted Practice Problem  
- A simply supported beam with two supports at its ends carries a uniformly distributed load across its entire length. Find the reactions at both supports.

7. Corrected Solution  
Step 1: Identify the system  
- Beam with two supports (let’s call them A and B) and a central point load P applied at midpoint.

Step 2: Define reactions  
- Let RA be the reaction force at support A.  
- Let RB be the reaction force at support B.

Step 3: Apply equilibrium equations  
- Sum of vertical forces = 0:  
  RA + RB - P = 0  → (1)

- Sum of moments about A = 0:  
  RB × L - P × (L/2) = 0  → (2)  
  where L is the length of the beam.

Step 4: Solve for RB from (2):  
  RB = (P × L/2) / L = P/2

Step 5: Substitute RB into (1):  
  RA + P/2 - P = 0 → RA = P - P/2 = P/2

Step 6: Conclusion  
- Both supports carry equal reactions of P/2 due to the central load.

Final answer:  
RA = RB = P/2
